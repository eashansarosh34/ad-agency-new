# Client Onboarding Checklist — Lead Gen Engine

Use this every time you sign a new client. Goal: go from signed contract to live campaign in under a week, without ever holding the client's money, passwords, or asset ownership.

---

## Step 0 — Info to collect on the first call

- [ ] Business name, location(s), and the one service they most want more customers for (e.g. "implants" not "dentistry")
- [ ] Current monthly enquiry volume (rough number, even if it's a guess)
- [ ] Average value of one new customer/patient — this is what makes cost-per-lead numbers mean something later
- [ ] Monthly ad budget they're comfortable with (₹15,000–20,000 minimum to make ads worth running — below that, ads alone won't generate enough data to optimize)
- [ ] Do they already have a Meta Business Manager / Google Ads account, or are you starting from zero?
- [ ] One WhatsApp number that can be dedicated to leads (not someone's personal phone they also use for family)

---

## Step 1 — Get account access (without taking ownership)

**Never ask a client to transfer ownership of their ad account, pixel, page, or domain to you.** Always use *partner access* — it's the standard, safe method, and it means a clean handoff if the relationship ever ends.

**Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Ads:**
- [ ] Confirm the client is an **Admin** of their own Meta Business Manager (if they don't have one, they create it free at business.facebook.com)
- [ ] Send them your Agency Business Manager ID
- [ ] Walk them through: *Business Settings → Partners → Add → Give a partner access to your assets* → paste your Business ID → select the ad account → grant **Advertiser** access (not Admin — you don't need billing control, and they shouldn't give it)
- [ ] Same flow for their Instagram account and Pixel (Analyst access on the pixel is enough)

**Google Ads:**
- [ ] Get their Google Ads Customer ID
- [ ] Send them your Manager Account (MCC) ID
- [ ] They go to *Tools & Settings → Access and Security → Managers* and accept your link request, granting **Standard** access

**WhatsApp Business:**
- [ ] Confirm the dedicated WhatsApp number from Step 0
- [ ] Set it up on your chosen automation tool (AiSensy / Wati) under the client's own WhatsApp Business Account — this also needs Meta Business verification, which can take 1–3 days, so kick this off *first*, not last

**Billing — keep this clean:**
- [ ] Ad spend is paid by the client directly to Meta/Google on their own card — you never touch it
- [ ] Your retainer is invoiced separately (GST invoice if registered) and paid by bank transfer or UPI on a fixed monthly date

---

## Step 2 — What you set up on your end (while access requests are pending)

- [ ] Duplicate the weekly report template and rename it for this client
- [ ] Create a client folder in your creative tool (AdCreative.ai / Canva) and drop in their logo, brand colors, and any existing photos
- [ ] Write the WhatsApp auto-reply script with Claude — first message should acknowledge instantly and ask the one qualifying question that matters most for this business (e.g. "Which service are you interested in?")
- [ ] Draft 4–6 first-round ad creative variations (mix of offer angles: discount, free consultation, before/after, urgency)

---

## Step 3 — Launch readiness (before the first rupee of ad spend)

- [ ] Lead form is live and tested — submit one yourself to confirm it reaches WhatsApp/email correctly
- [ ] WhatsApp auto-reply tested end to end
- [ ] Pixel/conversion tracking confirmed firing (test in Meta Events Manager)
- [ ] Client has confirmed their ad budget is loaded and a payment method is active on their ad account
- [ ] Client knows what week 1 will look like: expect some volatility while the algorithm is learning, real signal shows up around day 7–10

---

## Step 4 — Ongoing weekly cadence

- [ ] Day 7: send first weekly report, flag one thing that's working and one thing you're adjusting
- [ ] Every week after: refresh at least 1–2 creatives, even if performance is stable — fatigue compounds quietly
- [ ] Monthly: a short call or voice note, not just the report — this is the relationship part no AI agent replaces

---

### One reminder
Everything above takes you maybe 60–90 minutes of actual hands-on time per client to set up, spread over a few days while access requests process. That's the ceiling you're optimizing — not the tools, your own time per client.
